🔨Recent projects

QuadraSolve

Together with Simon Bohnen, I research how accessibility in education can be achieved with digitization. Because my iOS-app QuadraSolve got downloaded over 4,000 times on the App Store, Simon Bohnen ported in on Android and focused more on advanced features like the graph rather than accessibility. You can find its code open source on GitHub↗︎

AlpakAR coming soon

I'm firmly convinced that Augmented Reality will have enormous impact on the human life and therefore I was keen on getting to know ARKit. As one of the first ones to be awarded with a safe entry to the Hackathon Jugend Hackt↗︎ (~Youth Hacks), I created a simple iOS app that enables attendees to play in augmented reality, shoot pictures with the Hackathon's mascot (an alpaca) and share them with the community. You can find its code open source on GitHub↗︎.

Steering.Remote

In a team of 7 people at the Hackathon Code+Design↗︎, organised by ‎CODE University Berlin↗︎, we developed an autonomous driving truck based on an Arduino. Together with my friend Simon Bohnen↗︎ I was responsible of the wireless steering per iOS app. You can find its code open source on GitHub↗︎.

PiTime

Together with Robin Janke I developed a proprietary web application for video calls between two specifically configured raspberry pis with WebRTC and Node.js which is now used daily by BROCKHAUS AG↗︎ to communicate between different settlements.
